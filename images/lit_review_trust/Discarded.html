Communicating Risk

(from Intelligence Communication in the Digital Era)		IC-- The author points out that people, including decision-makers, “view risk differently because they process risk information based on their existing beliefs and values.”
-- Since now the “technology exists to create customized products for individual clients,” the author encourages analysts to not stop there, but rather also “adjust their products to diminish the risk perception gap,” meaning the difference between an analyst’s and their customer’s perception of risk.
-- The author argues that, while decision-making research shows that “emotional or affective-cognitive factors can influence the process of judgement in many ways,” most analytic products are focused entirely on the analytical side of the brain, as most analysts “see an appeal to emotion as evidence of a weak argument.” This emotional appeal is used in journalism; one common tactic involves “humanizing” a story.
-- “If a client has ‘low trust' in your analytic products, they are not inclined to believe what you have to say. No matter the revelation, the client unconsciously looks for reasons to disbelieve your findings.”
-- The author recommends that analysts use emotional appeals to “gently counterbalance the natural tendencies of the client” to reduce the risk perception gap.

^^ this gets into dicey territory of too heavily customizing analytical productsin a way that (1) compromises the product's integrity and (2) is unrealistic in its tailoring.
